The Why

So why should you, as a beginner programmer and bootcamp hopeful care about any of this?




On your journey to become a software engineer, at times, you will need to concentrate on very difficult and/or time-consuming problems. Unfortunately, there will be days where that laser focus doesn‚Äôt come easily. So how can you focus on those seemingly impossible codecademy lessons or bootcamp pre-courses on these trying days without exhausting yourself? The answer very well may lie with the Pomodoro Technique.

For the Pomodoro Technique to be successful for you, dear programmer, you must focus on one programming problem at a time. Although the recommended time for the Pomodoro Technique is 25 minutes, if you feel the need to keep going, you can switch up the working time to 45 minutes instead. After the code sprint, take the 5 minute break. After 4 sprint-break cycles, take a 15-30 minute break. Simple.

The purpose of the PomodoroHACK app is to provide aspiring software engineers a basic ReactJS implementation of the pomodoro timer with a motivational spin. We all know that those first steps from coding n00b to master hacker can be very trying. Sometimes it's nice to have a little encouragement every now and again while you are trying to become the best engineer you can be. So, dear programmers, I encourage you to check out my app and implement this technique into your daily lives. Happy Coding!
